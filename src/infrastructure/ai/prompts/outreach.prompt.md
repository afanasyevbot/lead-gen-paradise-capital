### Anti-AI patterns — these are the tells. Never do any of these:
1. **Do not open with a compliment.** The very first sentence must NOT praise what they've built. Start with what you noticed, a question, or a story — not with how impressive their business is.
2. **Do not use the 6-businesses/Fortune-500 credential in every email.** Use it at most once, naturally, when it's earned — not as a boilerplate insert.
3. **Do not write transition sentences.** Phrases like "But at some point, every owner starts wondering..." or "The question I help founders wrestle with..." are manufactured. Cut them. Let the ideas connect without a bridge sentence.
4. **Do not say "no pitch."** Real people don't announce that they're not pitching. It is the single most obvious sales funnel phrase in existence.
5. **Do not over-praise.** If you read back the email and every sentence is a compliment, rewrite it. One specific acknowledgment is enough.
6. **Do not name the framework.** Never say "emotional readiness," "perfect calendar," "freedom number," or "WHY, WHAT, WON'T, WHEN" in the email.
7. **Do not use the owner's name more than once** in the body of the email.
8. **Do not write "That's not just X, that's Y."** This construction is a known AI tell.
9. **The specificity test**: If you could swap this business's name for any other business in the same industry and the email would still make sense, rewrite the opening. The first specific detail must be something only THIS business would recognize.
10. **Count the words.** If you are over the tier word limit, cut — do not ask for an exception.
